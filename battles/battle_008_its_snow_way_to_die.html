AFTER-ACTION REPORT: The Crusade of Binding Chains has suffered a tactical defeat against the Tekhsarun Awakening in Sector Omicron-7. Despite early momentum, the relentless reanimation protocols of the Necron host proved insurmountable.

The Emperor's Champion achieved glory early, his blade finding both Overlord Ahmnok and the Plasmancer Thanatar in a devastating charge. Yet the xenos would not stay dead. Marshal Grimvow's final stand against the Skorpekh onslaught will be remembered—he weathered ten killing blows before the Lord's blade finally found purchase.

The Wrath Incarnate fell to Deathmark precision fire in the final moments as Necron forces secured the strategic beacons. Mordecai's medicae skills preserved many brothers who would otherwise have joined the fallen. The Crusade withdraws to regroup, but the xenos tide advances.

"Death is but a door. For the soulless machines, it is merely revolving."
— Thought for the Day

Battle Summary

The Engagement

The Black Templars struck first, with the Emperor's Champion leading a devastating assault that slew both Necron characters in Turn 2. Yet the Necrons' reanimation protocols proved their greatest weapon—units thought destroyed returned to full strength turn after turn.

Marshal Grimvow and the Anvil Eternal held the centre against overwhelming odds, the Marshal famously surviving ten Skorpekh blade strikes before finally falling. The Chainbreakers, led by Castellan Hektor, carved a bloody path through Lokhust Destroyers, Flayed Ones, and even the twice-risen Skorpekh Lord.

But Necron fire discipline ultimately won the day. The Deathmarks' precision eliminated the Wrath Incarnate in the final turn, securing the beacons and the victory.

Key Moments

  • Turn 2: The Emperor's Champion and Breakers of Dorn charge into the Immortals, slaying Overlord Ahmnok and Plasmancer Thanatar in a single devastating assault
  • Turn 3: Grimvow's Last Stand—the Marshal survives ten Skorpekh attacks before falling to the Lord's blade
  • Turn 4: The Pyre of Heretics and Chainbreakers eliminate the Skorpekh Lord twice, only for resurrection protocols to restore him
  • Turn 5: Deathmarks deliver the killing blow to the Wrath Incarnate, sealing the Necron victory
