Spoils of Decay

Supply Raid 3 — Nachmund Gauntlet

++ Priority: Crimson ++ ++ Source: Aeterna Noctis Sensorium ++ ++ Timestamp: 825.M42 ++

PRIORITY INTERCEPT: Orbital defence collapse in Sector Epsilon-7 has exposed a rich stockpile of vital equipment—munitions, fuel reserves, and archeotech components left unguarded in the chaos. Multiple forces converging on location.

The Bottomless Bucket expedition has identified potential STC fragments among the cache—data-wafers bearing markings consistent with Kentarchus Minoris manufacture patterns. The Eleven Mysteries may yet yield another secret.

But the Sons of Decay are already there. Their plague-touched hands reach for the same spoils, and where Nurgle's children tread, nothing remains untainted. The supplies themselves are time-limited—corrupted caches will be rendered worthless. Speed is essential. Contamination is inevitable.

This is a scramble for tainted treasure. The victor claims the spoils. The defeated claims only rot.

Mission Rule: Drop Zone Depleted

At the start of the first battle round, the players randomly select three different objective markers in No Man's Land:

Selection Designation Removed At
1st selected Alpha objective Start of Battle Round 3
2nd selected Beta objective Start of Battle Round 4
3rd selected Gamma objective Start of Battle Round 5
Objectives disappear as the battle progresses! Plan your movement carefully—a held objective may vanish before you score it.

Scoring: Secure Supplies

Progressive Objective — The more supplies that can be extracted safely from this site, the greater a success you will be credited with.

From the second battle round onwards, at the end of the Command phase, the player whose turn it is scores VP as follows:

Battle Round VP per Objective Controlled
Rounds 2 & 3 1 VP
Round 4 2 VP
Round 5 4 VP
Round 5 Exception: The player who has the second turn scores VP at the end of the turn instead of at the end of their Command phase.

Reinforcement Arrival Options

Units arriving from Tactical Reserves have three options for entering the battlefield:

1. Walk On (Standard Reserves)

  • Set up wholly within 6" of a battlefield edge
  • Must be more than 9" from all enemy models
  • Round 2: Own deployment zone or No Man's Land edges only
  • Round 3+: Any battlefield edge (including enemy deployment zone)

2. Surgical Deep Strike

  • Set up anywhere on the battlefield more than 3" from all enemy models
  • Must take a Deep Strike test (2D6, subtract 1 per enemy model within 9")
  • Test is always required, even if no enemies are nearby
  • Results range from acting normally (4+) to Deep Strike Mishap (1 or less)
High risk, high reward. A failed test can result in mortal wounds, Battle-shock, or worse.

3. Defend Home (Emergency Entry)

  • Set up each model within 1" of your own battlefield edge
  • Must be outside Engagement Range of all enemy models
  • Unit is automatically Battle-shocked
Use this to contest enemies threatening your deployment zone — arrive close to them at the cost of being Battle-shocked.
