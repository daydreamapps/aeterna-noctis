Hulk Battle 013

No Ground Given

Stranglehold 2 — Nachmund Gauntlet

++ Priority: Crimson ++ ++ Source: Aeterna Noctis Sensorium ++ ++ Timestamp: 827.M42 ++

EMERGENCY TACTICAL UPDATE: Drop assault in Sector Kappa-7 has devolved into close-quarters chaos. Both Imperial and traitor forces scattered across multiple decks. Command structure fractured. Vox discipline minimal.

The Crusade of Binding Chains advances through the wreckage, Marshal Grimvow's voice cutting through static with the clarity of a tolling bell. They will not yield. They cannot yield. Every metre of deck plating must be held or taken—there is no middle ground against the servants of the Dark Gods.

The Killing Stone emerges from the shadows with murder in their hearts. These Heretic Astartes know that whoever consolidates their position first will control this engagement. Neither side can afford to give an inch.

Recommend all non-combatants evacuate adjacent sectors. This will be settled blade to blade.

Mission Overview

In the wake of a chaotic drop assault, invaders and defenders alike are scattered. The first commander to secure a decisive and commanding position on this battlefield will be able to drive their foes before them and consolidate their strength.

Board Size 60" x 44"
Objectives 5 Total
Deployment Hammer and Anvil

Deployment Zones

Mission Rules

Staging Post

Players only gain 1CP during their turn if they control the objective marker within their deployment zone.

Critical: If you do not control your deployment zone objective, you do not gain CP at the start of your Command phase. Protect your staging post!

Scoring

Progressive Objective: Ruthless Aggression

There is no time for complacency in this fight. Do not give the foe an inch!

At the end of your turn:

Condition VP
Control one or more objective markers that were controlled by your opponent at the start of the turn 1 VP
If one or more of those objective markers were within your opponent's deployment zone +1 VP

Progressive Objective: Domination

Establish swift strategic dominance to deny your foes the chance to consolidate their position.

From Round 2 onwards, at the end of your Command phase:

Condition VP
Control one or more objective markers 1 VP
Control more objective markers than your opponent 2 VP
Round 5 Exception: The player with the second turn scores at the end of their turn instead of their Command phase.

Victor Bonus

The victor can select one additional unit from their Crusade army to be Marked for Greatness.

This means the victor may Mark for Greatness two units in total after this battle (the standard one plus the bonus).

Army Setup: Tactical Reserves

Nachmund Gauntlet uses Tactical Reserves — your army is split into waves that arrive throughout the battle.

Incursion (1000pts) Wave Configuration

Wave Points Limit Arrives
Primary Wave Up to 500 pts Deployment (start of battle)
Reinforcement Wave 1 Up to 250 pts Start of Battle Round 2
Reinforcement Wave 2 Up to 250 pts Start of Battle Round 3
Your total army can exceed 1000pts — the wave limits are separate. Plan your Order of Battle accordingly.

Reinforcement Arrival Options

When a Reinforcement Wave arrives, each unit in that wave can arrive via one of the following methods:

  • Battlefield Edge Arrival: Set up wholly within 6" of your battlefield edge and more than 9" from enemy models
  • Deep Strike (if unit has ability): Use the unit's Deep Strike ability as normal, arriving more than 9" from enemy models
  • Surgical Deep Strike (1CP Stratagem): One unit per wave can Deep Strike 3" closer (6" from enemies instead of 9"). Must have Deep Strike ability.

Post-Battle Reporting

After the battle, report the following to your Game Master:

Final Score Your VP total and your opponent's VP total
Kills Which of your units destroyed which enemy units (for "Dealers of Death" XP: +1 XP per 3 units destroyed)
Casualties Which of your units were destroyed (they must make Out of Action tests)
XP Earned Per unit: +1 XP (participation) + Agenda XP + Dealers of Death + Marked for Greatness (if applicable)
Notable Moments 2-3 memorable events for the battle report (heroic last stands, dramatic kills, lucky/unlucky dice, etc.)
