Nachmund Gauntlet — Heralds of Vengeance

Battle Briefing

++ Priority: Crimson ++ ++ Source: Warp-Resonance Detection Grid ++ ++ Timestamp: 820.M42 — April 1, 2026 ++

The Farseers of Bahzhakhain Süirel have glimpsed something terrible in the skeins of fate. Deep within the Aeterna Noctis, ancient teleportation arrays lie dormant—relics of a forgotten age when the Hulk was many ships, not one twisted mass. Whoever activates these systems first will gain a devastating advantage in the war to come.

The Killing Stone has learned of these arrays through darker means. Yubaba's sorcerers have communed with entities that whisper of power untapped, of strike beacons that could deliver their warriors directly into the heart of enemy territory. The Chaos Lord has dispatched his swiftest forces to claim these relics before the Aeldari can secure them.

Both forces converge on the same junction. The Aeldari move with desperate purpose—their visions show only death if they fail. The Chaos Space Marines advance with cruel confidence, their Raptors already circling in the darkness above. Strike beacons must be guided, triangulated, locked onto enemy positions.

Whoever succeeds will herald vengeance upon their foes. Whoever fails will find that same vengeance delivered unto them.

Mission Rules

Guide Strike Beacon (Action)

Aspect Details
Starts Your Shooting phase
Units One or more units from your Crusade army, each within range of a different objective marker that you control
Completes End of your turn
Effect For each unit that completes this Action, you can move the objective marker that unit is within range of up to 6". That objective marker cannot end that move on top of any other objective marker or model.

Victor Bonus

The victor's Crusade force gains +1 RP and +1 SAP.

Scoring

Progressive Objective: Strike Triangulation Achieved

From Round 2 onwards, at the end of your turn:

Objective Marker Position VP
Wholly within opponent's deployment zone 3 VP each
Wholly within 6" of opponent's deployment zone 2 VP each
Wholly within 12" of opponent's deployment zone 1 VP each
These VP values are not cumulative. Each objective marker scores based on only its closest qualifying position.

Army Setup: Tactical Reserves

Nachmund Gauntlet uses Tactical Reserves — your army is split into waves that arrive throughout the battle.

Incursion (1000pts) Wave Configuration

Wave Points Limit Arrives
Primary Wave Up to 400 pts Deployment (start of battle)
Reinforcement Wave 1 Up to 300 pts Start of Battle Round 2
Reinforcement Wave 2 Up to 300 pts Start of Battle Round 3
Your total army can exceed 1000pts — the wave limits are separate. Plan your Order of Battle accordingly.

Building Your Waves

  • Assign each unit to a wave before the battle
  • Reinforcement waves arrive from your battlefield edge (or via Deep Strike if the unit has that ability)
  • You choose which reinforcement wave arrives at the start of each round

Post-Battle Reporting

After the battle, report the following to your Game Master:

Final Score Your VP total and your opponent's VP total
Kills Which of your units destroyed which enemy units (for "Dealers of Death" XP: +1 XP per 3 units destroyed)
Casualties Which of your units were destroyed (they must make Out of Action tests)
XP Earned Per unit: +1 XP (participation) + Agenda XP + Dealers of Death + Marked for Greatness (if applicable)
Notable Moments 2-3 memorable events for the battle report (heroic last stands, dramatic kills, lucky/unlucky dice, etc.)
