Eye of the Storm

Custom Mission — The Twin Pillars

++ Priority: Vermillion ++ ++ Source: Aeterna Noctis Sensorium ++ ++ Timestamp: 829.M42 ++

ANOMALY DETECTED: Two xenos monoliths of unknown origin have been identified in Sector Omicron-4. These ancient pillars rise nearly 24 metres from the deck, their summits crackling with dormant energies. Between them, a strange fog churns—sensors unable to penetrate its depths.

The Judgement of Aldurukh moves to secure the site. The Dark Angels speak of "ancient secrets" that must not fall into unworthy hands. Their true motives, as always, remain shrouded.

The Trans-Eternians Alliance approaches from the opposite vector. The Kin have logged this location in their Votann—these pillars contain mineral deposits of immense value. A Grudge will be marked if they are denied their claim.

Two forces. Two pillars. One shrouded battlefield between them. The storm gathers.

Mission Overview

Two ancient xenos pillars rise from the depths of the Aeterna Noctis, their summits crackling with strange energies. Between them, an impenetrable fog churns—providing sanctuary to those within, but obscuring all sight. Both forces seek to control the high ground and dominate the shrouded battlefield below.

Board Size 60" x 44"
Tower Height ~24" each
Tower Separation ~24" apart
Objectives 5 Total

Battlefield Setup

Tower Placement

Two towers placed approximately 24" apart, creating a central corridor between them.

Objectives

  • Tower Summits: 1 objective at the top of each tower (2 total)
  • Shrouded by Fog Zone: 3 objectives at ground level between the towers

The Shrouded by Fog Zone

The entire ground-level area between the two towers (within 12" of the centre line connecting them).

Special Rules

Shrouded by Fog

The strange energies between the pillars create a dense, concealing mist that confounds targeting systems and offers strange protection.

INFANTRY and MOUNTED units wholly within this zone gain:

  • Stealth: -1 to hit when targeted by ranged attacks
  • Benefit of Cover: +1 to saving throws against ranged attacks
  • 4+ Invulnerable Save: Energy interference protects them

VEHICLE units wholly within this zone gain:

  • Stealth: -1 to hit when targeted by ranged attacks
  • Benefit of Cover: +1 to saving throws against ranged attacks
Vehicles do NOT gain the 4+ invulnerable save—the energy field cannot fully encompass their mass.

Plunging Fire

From the heights, warriors rain death upon those below.

Models on the towers gain +1 to hit (ranged attacks only) when targeting units at ground level.

Precarious Position

The ancient stone offers little purchase, and the distracted or panicked may find themselves plummeting to their doom.

Any unit that is Battle-shocked and has one or more models within 1" of a ledge must immediately take a Balance Test for each such model:

  • Roll a D6. On a 1-2, that model falls and is slain (no saves of any kind).
  • This roll can be re-rolled by spending 1 CP.
Characters, elite units, and valuable models should think twice before standing near the edge!

Vertiginous Heights

A shot at the wrong moment can send a warrior tumbling into the void.

Any unit that is hit by a ranged attack while one or more of its models are within 1" of a ledge must immediately take a Battle-shock test after the attacking unit finishes its attacks.

This test suffers a -1 modifier for every 6" of height off the ground:

Height Modifier
6" high -1
12" high -2
18" high -3
24" high -4
A unit only takes one Vertiginous Heights Battle-shock test per phase, regardless of how many times it is hit.

Vertical Movement

Units can climb towers at half their Movement characteristic (measure vertically).

  • FLY and JUMP PACK units move normally.
  • Tower summits can hold a maximum of 10 models.

Deep Strike Restrictions

Units cannot arrive via Deep Strike within 6" horizontally of a tower base (energy interference).

Units with Deep Strike can arrive on tower platforms if they fit within the model limit.

Tower Deployment

Special Rule: Up to half of your Primary Wave (by points) may be deployed on your designated tower instead of your deployment zone. Models must fit within the tower's model limit (10 models per summit).
Each player is assigned one tower at the start of the game (roll off or agree). You may only deploy on YOUR tower during initial deployment.

Scoring

Progressive Objective: Fog Dominance

Control the shrouded battlefield to claim victory.

At the end of your turn:

Condition VP
Control one or more objectives in the Shrouded by Fog zone 1 VP
Control more Fog objectives than your opponent 1 VP

Progressive Objective: Summit Control

The high ground commands the battlefield.

At the end of each battle round:

Condition VP
Control one tower summit 1 VP
Control BOTH tower summits 3 VP
Controlling both summits awards 3 VP total (not 1 + 3).

Victor Bonus

The victor can select one additional unit from their Crusade army to be Marked for Greatness.

This means the victor may Mark for Greatness two units in total after this battle (the standard one plus the bonus).

Post-Battle Reporting

After the battle, report the following to your Game Master:

  • Final Score: Your VP total and your opponent's VP total
  • Kills: Which of your units destroyed which enemy units
  • Casualties: Which of your units were destroyed (they must make Out of Action tests)
  • Falls: Any models that fell from the towers (for narrative glory!)
  • Notable Moments: 2-3 memorable events for the battle report
