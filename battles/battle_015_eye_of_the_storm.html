AFTER-ACTION REPORT: The Judgement of Aldurukh has secured Sector Omicron-4. The xenos monoliths are now under Dark Angels control. Votann forces have withdrawn.

The mists between the pillars proved treacherous for both forces—limited visibility forced close-quarters engagement. The Sicaran Venator "Mortis Tenebrae" scored critical hits on the Hekaton Land Fortress, finishing the xenos vehicle with a devastating tank shock that left both craft in ruins. Murder Command Varangian—the Ravenwing Command Squad—broke free and systematically hunted both Pioneer units, reaping a heavy toll.

Judiciar Mordecai Astrovel emerged from shadow in the battle's climax, charging the Kahl Warlord at the central objective and taking the enemy commander's head. With Votann command structure eliminated, both forces consolidated—each holding a monolith, but the Dark Angels claiming strategic dominance.

The Vanguard Veterans and Ravenwing Command Squad have been Marked for Greatness. The Lion's sons hold the high ground.

Battle Narrative

The Thunderkyn and Jaegers faced off against Brother Konor and the Seekers of Retribution atop opposing monoliths. For two turns, the obscuring mists limited exchanges to scattered shots, though Brother Konor weathered sustained fire from the Hekaton.

Turn three saw Mortis Tenebrae score three critical hits on the Hekaton, leaving it severely wounded before finishing the Votann tank with a heroic tank shock—the explosion injuring both the Sicaran and the Brohyr Iron-master. Outflanking deep strikes by the Einhyr and Bladeguard Companions sowed chaos in both back lines.

The Einhyr Champion survived overwatch—which proved more deadly to Squad Evasto's Hellblasters, who lost three to "Gets Hot"—before tossing the Assault Intercessors aside like ragdolls. In response, Murder Command Varangian broke free and systematically hunted both Pioneer units, destroying one utterly.

A daring counter-push saw Beserks charging the Sicaran while the Kahl's unit defiantly claimed the central objective. The Sword Companions ended the Iron-master and all but one Beserk. With the Votann command incapacitated and both sides holding a monolith each, the mists concealed the bodies of the fallen.

Final moment: Judiciar Mordecai Astrovel stepped from shadow, saw the Kahl Warlord, and charged—taking the head from the Votann force and securing Guardian victory.

Special Rules

Shrouded by Fog

The strange energies between the pillars create a dense, concealing mist that confounds targeting systems and offers strange protection.

INFANTRY and MOUNTED units wholly within this zone gain:

  • Stealth: -1 to hit when targeted by ranged attacks
  • Benefit of Cover: +1 to saving throws against ranged attacks
  • 4+ Invulnerable Save: Energy interference protects them

VEHICLE units wholly within this zone gain:

  • Stealth: -1 to hit when targeted by ranged attacks
  • Benefit of Cover: +1 to saving throws against ranged attacks
Vehicles do NOT gain the 4+ invulnerable save—the energy field cannot fully encompass their mass.

Plunging Fire

From the heights, warriors rain death upon those below.

Models on the towers gain +1 to hit (ranged attacks only) when targeting units at ground level.

Precarious Position

The ancient stone offers little purchase, and the distracted or panicked may find themselves plummeting to their doom.

Any unit that is Battle-shocked and has one or more models within 1" of a ledge must immediately take a Balance Test for each such model:

  • Roll a D6. On a 1-2, that model falls and is slain (no saves of any kind).
  • This roll can be re-rolled by spending 1 CP.
Characters, elite units, and valuable models should think twice before standing near the edge!

Vertiginous Heights

A shot at the wrong moment can send a warrior tumbling into the void.

Any unit that is hit by a ranged attack while one or more of its models are within 1" of a ledge must immediately take a Battle-shock test after the attacking unit finishes its attacks.

This test suffers a -1 modifier for every 6" of height off the ground:

Height Modifier
6" high -1
12" high -2
18" high -3
24" high -4
A unit only takes one Vertiginous Heights Battle-shock test per phase, regardless of how many times it is hit.

Vertical Movement

Units can climb towers at half their Movement characteristic (measure vertically).

  • FLY and JUMP PACK units move normally.
  • Tower summits can hold a maximum of 10 models.

Deep Strike Restrictions

Units cannot arrive via Deep Strike within 6" horizontally of a tower base (energy interference).

Units with Deep Strike can arrive on tower platforms if they fit within the model limit.

Scoring

Progressive Objective: Fog Dominance

Control the shrouded battlefield to claim victory.

At the end of your turn:

Condition VP
Control one or more objectives in the Shrouded by Fog zone 1 VP
Control more Fog objectives than your opponent 1 VP

Progressive Objective: Summit Control

The high ground commands the battlefield.

At the end of each battle round:

Condition VP
Control one tower summit 1 VP
Control BOTH tower summits 3 VP
Controlling both summits awards 3 VP total (not 1 + 3).

Victor Bonus

The victor can select one additional unit from their Crusade army to be Marked for Greatness.

This means the victor may Mark for Greatness two units in total after this battle (the standard one plus the bonus).
