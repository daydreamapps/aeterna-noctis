# Phase 3 — The Closing Labyrinth (DRAFT — NOT PUBLISHED)

**Status:** Awaiting user approval. Do not publish until Phase 2 wrap-up and points are finalized.

**Why this exists:** Original Phase 3 was a 6-week continuation in the vein of Phases 1–2 with new powers/paths. After 12 weeks of campaign, player concentration is falling off and certain armies (notably Death Guard, Black Templars) have become overpowered. Two players have already brought in second armies (Templar → Admech, Death Guard → Orks) to address balance. The new Phase 3 is a tight, tournament-flavored finale designed to give the campaign a clean, dramatic close.

---

## Format Overview

- **Duration:** 2 weeks (down from 6).
- **Total battles:** 6 + 1 optional grand finale.
- **Structure:** Alliance vs Alliance, round-robin. Each pairing plays 2 battles.
  - Guardians vs Despoilers — 2 battles
  - Guardians vs Marauders — 2 battles
  - Despoilers vs Marauders — 2 battles
- **Per alliance:** 4 battles total (2 against each rival alliance). Each alliance must nominate **two different players** across its battles in a given pairing — the same player cannot fight both battles of the same matchup.
- **Scheduling:** No central deadline — teams coordinate their own battle dates within the 2-week window.
- **Forfeit:** If a nominated player cannot field within the 2-week window, that battle is recorded as **missed** for the absent team (0 CVP — see scoring).

## Mission & Game Rules

- **Edition:** 10th Edition, standard tournament rules.
- **Points:** 2,000 pts per side.
- **Reserves cap:** 500 pts (standard competitive cap).
- **Mission:** *Purge the Foe* (confirmed). Primary scoring:
  - **Any round** (end of battle round): 4VP if one or more enemy units were destroyed this round.
  - **Round 2+** (end of battle round): 4VP if more enemy units than friendly units were destroyed this round.
  - **Round 2+** (end of your Command phase, or end of your turn if it is round 5 and you are going second): 4VP if you control ≥1 objective marker, +4VP if you control more objectives than your opponent.
  - **No secondaries.** Primary scoring only.
- **No Crusade shenanigans:** No additional waves, no campaign sub-rules, no extra phase-specific mechanics. This is a clean tournament game.
- **Carry-overs that DO apply:**
  - All unit upgrades, traits, and battle honours earned in Phases 1–2.
  - Any Hulk bonuses your alliance earned in Phases 1–2.
  - **Crusade Blessings of the Omnissiah** based on the standard Crusade Points (CP) difference between the two armies — applied as per the core Crusade rules.

## Army List Construction

Lists are built from your **current Crusade roster** under the following rules:

1. **Select up to 2,000 pts** of units from your existing army roster.
2. **Units not selected are treated as DESTROYED in-narrative.** They did not make it to the final battle.
   - Their accumulated **experience may be reallocated** onto surviving units (and *only* their experience — not their traits or specific honours).
3. **New units may be added** to fill out the 2,000 pt list. These join as fresh additions.
4. **Thematic upgrades are permitted** where supported by the codex / campaign rules. Examples:
   - Gravis-armoured infantry → Terminators (carry experience over; pay the new points cost).
   - Smaller tank → larger tank of the same chassis family.
   - Transports → battle tanks where lore-appropriate.
   - **Spirit of the rule:** keep upgrades thematic and not wildly impactful. If in doubt, ask the Campaign Master before locking the list.
5. **Reallocating XP from destroyed units:** if you want to debut or level up a specific unit, you can sacrifice an existing one and dump its XP into the new/upgraded unit.

## Campaign Scoring (CVP per Battle)

Each Phase 3 battle awards alliance CVP as follows:

| Result | CVP |
|---|---|
| Win | **4** |
| Draw | **2** |
| Loss | **1** |
| Missed / Forfeited | **0** |

A loss is still worth showing up for. A forfeit is not.

## Survivor Tracking

- After each battle, **track which units survived** (i.e. were not destroyed during the game).
- These survivors are eligible for the **Grand Finale** (see below).

## The Grand Finale (Optional, For Fun)

- At the end of the 6 battles, a **three-way battle** between the surviving units of each alliance's chosen forces.
- **Eligibility:** only units that survived a Phase 3 battle can be fielded.
- **Caveat:** if an alliance's third army never participated in a Phase 3 battle, none of its units are eligible for the finale. Unfortunate but necessary.
- This battle is **for narrative and fun** — final points/CVP impact TBD.
