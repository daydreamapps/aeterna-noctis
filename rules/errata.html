Clarification Rules Adaptations — Reinforcement Restrictions
Added: April 2026

In Nachmund Gauntlet battles the following adaptations apply:

Rapid Ingress Prohibited

Players cannot use the Rapid Ingress Stratagem.

Battlefield Edge Deployment Under Pressure

Each time you set up a unit that is arriving from Reserves, unless that unit is performing a Surgical Deep Strike, you can set that unit up within 1" of your own battlefield edge even if enemy units are within 9" of that unit.

  • Each model from that unit must be set up within 1" of your battlefield edge
  • The unit cannot be set up within Engagement Range of any enemy units
  • Penalty: A unit set up on the battlefield in this way is automatically Battle-shocked
Tactical Note: This allows reinforcements to arrive even when enemy forces are screening your board edge, but at the cost of being Battle-shocked until your next Command phase.

Clarification Reinforcement Wave Arrival Options
Added: April 2026

Question: How do units from Reinforcement Waves arrive on the battlefield?

Answer: When a Reinforcement Wave arrives at the start of a Battle Round, each unit in that wave can arrive using one of the following methods:

Method Requirements Placement
Battlefield Edge Any unit Wholly within 6" of your battlefield edge and more than 9" from enemy models
Deep Strike Unit must have Deep Strike ability As per the unit's Deep Strike rules (typically more than 9" from enemy models)
Surgical Deep Strike Unit must have Deep Strike ability + 1CP Stratagem As Deep Strike, but only needs to be more than 6" from enemy models
Surgical Deep Strike Limit: Only one unit per Reinforcement Wave can use this Stratagem.

Source: Wahapedia — Tactical Reserves

House Ruling Close-Range Deep Strike Abilities
Added: April 2026

Issue: Some units have abilities that allow them to Deep Strike closer than 9" from enemy models. This conflicts with the Surgical Deep Strike stratagem, which already provides a similar benefit.

Ruling: Any unit with a rule that permits Deep Strike closer than 9" has that ability modified for this campaign:

When performing a Surgical Deep Strike, this unit may ignore up to X enemy models within 9" when calculating the difficulty of the Surgical Deep Strike test, where X equals the number of inches closer than 9" their original ability permitted.

Calculating the Bonus

Original Ability Inches Saved Models Ignored
Deep Strike outside 6" (instead of 9") 3" Ignore up to 3 models
Deep Strike outside 3" (instead of 9") 6" Ignore up to 6 models

Example

Incursor Squad has an ability that allows them to Deep Strike outside of 6" instead of 9". Under this ruling:

  • The Incursors cannot use this ability to deploy at 6" normally — they must use Surgical Deep Strike like everyone else
  • When performing a Surgical Deep Strike test, they may ignore up to 3 enemy models within 9" when calculating the test difficulty
  • This represents their superior infiltration training making the surgical insertion easier
Design Intent: This preserves the Surgical Deep Strike stratagem as the only way to deploy closer than 9" while still rewarding units that have specialised infiltration abilities.
House Ruling Phase-End BP & SAP Allocation — Location Control
Added: April 2026

At the conclusion of each Campaign Phase, alliances compete for control of Strategic Sites aboard the Aeterna Noctis through a sealed two-round allocation process. This process is adapted from the Nachmund Gauntlet Campaign Phase rules.

Round 1 — Battle Points (BP)

BP earned during the phase is public information. Each alliance privately informs the Campaign Master of how they wish to allocate their BP across the contested Strategic Sites. Alliances may factor in what they know of rivals' BP totals when deciding their bids.

  • Allocations are submitted in secret — rivals do not see your distribution until results are announced
  • Once submitted, BP allocations are locked and cannot be changed
  • The Campaign Master collects all allocations, then publicly announces the results, revealing each alliance's distribution and the outcome for each site

Round 2 — Strategic Asset Points (SAP)

SAP totals are private information — alliances do not know how much SAP their rivals hold. Following the BP announcement, each alliance privately informs the Campaign Master of how they wish to allocate their SAP.

  • Allocations are submitted in secret
  • Once all alliances have submitted, the Campaign Master declares the final results, revealing SAP distributions, updated Control Levels for each Strategic Site, and any rewards granted
Strategic Note: Because BP is public but SAP is hidden, Round 1 creates an open bidding game while Round 2 is a blind gamble. An alliance that appears weak in BP may still hold decisive SAP — and vice versa.
Round Resource Visibility Announcement
1 Battle Points (BP) Public totals, secret allocation Campaign Master announces all results simultaneously
2 Strategic Asset Points (SAP) Private totals, secret allocation Campaign Master declares final control levels & rewards
