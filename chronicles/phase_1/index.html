Phase I

The First Tide of Blood

Vox-Intercept // Allocation Report

Transmitted: End of Phase I

As the first phase of the war for the Aeterna Noctis drew to a close, the three alliances committed their forces in a display of diverging priorities. The Despoilers moved with grim certainty upon the Bridge of Sovereignty, deploying unopposed — their grip on the ship's nerve-centre now tightening by two control levels. The Guardians, meanwhile, cast only a token force toward the Vox-Relay Array, yet with none to contest them, that modest commitment is enough to secure a foothold there. Most striking was the savage three-way contest that erupted around Hangar Deck "Tertius" — all three alliances funnelling forces into the launch bays with desperate urgency. The Despoilers edge out the Guardians, earning themselves a two-level gain, while the Guardians claw back one level of their own. The Marauders' commitment, though substantial, proves insufficient to register any predicted gain. Perhaps most telling of all is the silence around the Nova Cannon Spires: not a single warrior was committed by any alliance, leaving those ancient gun decks cold, dark, and unclaimed. Whether this was oversight or cold calculation remains unclear — for the machinations of hidden agents on all sides are yet to be known.

Vox-Intercept // Secret Actions Resolved

Transmitted: Phase I — Final Resolution

The dust of battle had barely settled when the true theatre of war revealed itself — fought not with bolter and blade, but with ciphers, hidden coin, and shadow-pacts struck in the corroded bowels of the Aeterna Noctis. The Guardians, so certain of their cunning, had laid their stratagem upon Counter-Strategies — a ploy to mirror and outmanoeuvre their foes, claiming what others had seized. Yet their overcomplicated machinations collapsed beneath the weight of their own ambition. Gaining the least Strategic Asset Points of any alliance, the loyalists were left bewildered, unable to counter the strategies of the despicable Despoilers or the inscrutable Marauders. Their scheming bore no fruit.

The Despoilers, by contrast, moved with the blunt certainty of those who do not scheme — they simply strike. Committing their hidden resources to the Bridge of Sovereignty in a Decisive First Strike, they poured more SAP into that cursed command throne than all others combined, and claimed it utterly.

Most striking was the silence of the Marauders throughout the battles — weak in open war, they proved themselves masters of the shadows. Seven Strategic Asset Points funnelled into the Nova Cannon Spires in a single decisive action, overwhelming the paltry contributions of both other alliances. The ancient gun decks, silent no longer, answer now to the Marauders alone.
